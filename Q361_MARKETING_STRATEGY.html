Sales Strategy & Funnel

Sales Model

Freemium + Self-Service SaaS Model

Strategy: Low-barrier entry with 14-day free trial + self-serve onboarding, then account management for upsells and expansion. Target both self-service and enterprise sales motion simultaneously.

Sales Funnel Stages

Stage Activity Duration Conversion Rate Target
Awareness Content marketing, ads, partnerships, events Ongoing 10% → Consideration
Consideration Website, demo request, comparison pages, case studies 1-2 weeks 20% → Trial
Trial 14-day free trial, onboarding calls, email support 14 days 25% → Purchase
Purchase Sales demo, negotiation, contract, implementation 2-4 weeks 70% → Paid Customer
Implementation Setup, training, data migration, go-live support 4-6 weeks 95% → Active User
Retention & Growth Support, quarterly reviews, upsells, expansion Ongoing 90% retention → 15% expansion

Sales Channels

1. Direct Sales (Enterprise)

  • Target: Companies 500-10,000+ employees
  • Sales team size: 2-3 sales reps per region
  • Account value: $2,000-10,000/year
  • Sales cycle: 6-12 weeks
  • Success metric: 1-2 deals/rep/quarter

2. Self-Service / Free Trial (SMB)

  • Target: Companies 50-500 employees
  • Driven by website traffic and content
  • Account value: $200-2,000/year
  • Sales cycle: Self-serve, 1-2 days decision
  • Success metric: 15-20% trial-to-paid conversion

3. Channel Partners

  • HR consultants and implementation partners
  • Payroll and accounting software integrations
  • LinkedIn/Microsoft ecosystem partners
  • Revenue share: 15-20% commission

Sales Process

  1. Lead Generation: Website, content, ads, referrals
  2. Qualification: Discovery call, pain point confirmation
  3. Demo: Live product demo tailored to their needs
  4. Trial: 14-day free trial with dedicated support
  5. Negotiation: Pricing, customization, contract terms
  6. Close: Contract execution and payment
  7. Onboarding: Implementation and training
  8. Success: Quarterly business reviews, support

Pricing Strategy

Per-Employee SaaS Model

Pricing based on number of employees (active users) with minimum commitments. This aligns pricing with customer growth and reduces enterprise negotiation.
